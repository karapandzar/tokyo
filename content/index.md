## Places to visit

### The Tokyo Metropolitan Museum of Photography
opened in a temporary building in 1990 and moved to its current building in Yebisu Garden Place in 1995. At that time, it was one of the first photography galleries in Japan not to be dedicated to the works of a single photographer. Most of the exhibitions since then have been themed rather than devoted to a single photographer, but exhibitions have been dedicated to such photographers of the past as Berenice Abbott (1990) and Tadahiko Hayashi (1993–94), and also to living photographers including Martin Parr (2007) and Hiromi Tsuchida (2008).
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://maps.app.goo.gl/RyhU1XeknfCeTRDm6)
- **Wikipedia:** [Link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokyo_Photographic_Art_Museum)

### Shibuya Sky and Shibuya Crossing
Shibuya Sky is a stunning rooftop observatory atop the Shibuya Scramble Square building, offering panoramic views of Tokyo’s skyline, especially magical at sunset or night. Needs to be booked few days in advance. Just below lies the iconic Shibuya Crossing, one of the world’s busiest pedestrian intersections, where hundreds cross from all directions in a mesmerizing, choreographed flow. Together, they capture the energy and spectacle of modern Tokyo.
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://maps.app.goo.gl/PBQuQoywShzd9B4WA)

## Moving around Tokyo (works allover Japan)

### SUICA
is a mobile app that lets you ride Tokyo trains, subways, and buses with just your phone, no tickets or cards needed. Add Suica to your phone, top it up to start with with 2000JPY (12EUR), and tap at ticket gates to travel. It also works for small purchases at shops and vending machines.  
- [iOS (Apple Wallet)](https://apps.apple.com/jp/app/suica/id1156875272)  
- [Android (via Google Wallet or Suica app)](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.felicanetworks.mfm.main)

## Things to try

When in Tokyo, we should not miss the surprisingly delicious food at convenience stores like 7-Eleven or Family Mart, especially the egg sandwich and Famichiki from Family Mart (significantly better than KFC or anything similar). The creamy egg salad sandwich is a local favorite, and the hot, juicy Famichicken is a must-try snack. Perfect for a quick bite between sightseeing stops. Not comparable to the US equivalents. Onigiri with tuna is also not to miss.

## Live music spots
### Blue Note Tokyo
Blue Note Tokyo, in the stylish Aoyama district, is one of Tokyo’s premier live jazz venues with world-class performers and an elegant dining‑plus‑music experience.
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://goo.gl/maps/hdKq9X6J2sK2)
- **To reserve:**  [Link](https://www.tablecheck.com/en/shops/bluenoteplace/reserve?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### Alfie Jazz House (Roppongi)
Alfie is a small, cozy jazz bar tucked in the Hama Roppongi Building (5F), just steps from Roppongi Station Exit 1A. With seating for only ~40 guests, the vibe is intimate and personal.
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://maps.app.goo.gl/e6BsrFMYBi3b2LY87)
- Reservations: only by phone **+81‑3‑3479‑2037

![[1_3reagg6vb3bu6y6iy75pd.jpg|600]]


### Basement Bar
Nestled in the basement of a low-profile building in Shimokitazawa, Basement Bar is a gritty, intimate live house cherished by Tokyo’s underground scene. Shielded behind a liquor shop and tucked off the main street, it regularly hosts energetic indie rock and blues‑inspired sets featuring both local and international acts. This small venue delivers an authentic, visceral experience, loud music, close to the stage, no-frills setup.
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://maps.app.goo.gl/B9YrcBJ6njgDaXj36)

![[image.jpg|600]]

### Three
Just next door to Basement Bar, Three occupies its own basement spot in Shimokitazawa and is a beloved hub for indie, alternative rock, shoegaze, synth‑pop, and electronic‑rock performers. As one of the more affordable live houses in the area, it draws a creative local crowd and hosts diverse, cutting‑edge acts in a relaxed yet atmospheric setting.
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://maps.app.goo.gl/9vyD2RXXyiTrKzcN9)

### Shinjuku Pit Inn
is a legendary jazz club in Tokyo, known for its intimate atmosphere and world-class live performances. Tucked away in Shinjuku, it’s a must-visit for jazz lovers, hosting both Japanese and international artists. Shows run daily, and the evening sets offer an unforgettable Tokyo music experience.
- **Google maps:** [Link](https://maps.app.goo.gl/96qRdgWd8881hGZP6)
